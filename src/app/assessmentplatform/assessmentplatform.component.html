Rules and Regulations for Conducting Online Assessments

To ensure the smooth and fair execution of online assessments, the following rules and regulations must be strictly adhered to:

1. General Guidepnes

1.1 Students must log in at least 15 minutes before the scheduled start time to avoid any delays.

1.2 A stable internet connection is mandatory throughout the duration of the exam.

1.3 The assessment must be completed within the allocated 1-hour time limit. No extensions will be granted.

2. Technical Requirements

2.1 Students must use a laptop/desktop with a working camera and microphone. Mobile phones are not recommended unless specified.

2.2 Ensure the system has a compatible browser (Google Chrome or Mozilla Firefox) and the necessary plugins/software installed.

2.3 In case of technical issues during the exam, students must immediately notify the technical support team provided by the organizers.

3. Exam Environment

3.1 Students must choose a quiet and well-pt environment to avoid distractions.

3.2 Use of headphones or earphones is not allowed unless expcitly permitted.

3.3 No external help or collaboration with others is allowed during the assessment.

4. Integrity and Proctoring

4.1 The exam will be monitored through online proctoring tools to ensure fairness.

4.2 Any suspicious activity, such as switching screens, leaving the test window, or interacting with others, will result in disqualification.

4.3 Students must keep their cameras on and their microphones unmuted throughout the assessment.

5. Permitted and Prohibited Materials

5.1 Students are allowed to use a pen and blank paper for rough work if required.

5.2 The use of calculators, mobile phones, smartwatches, or any other electronic devices is strictly prohibited.

5.3 Browsing external websites or using reference materials during the exam is not allowed.

6.Attendance & Participation

6.1 Students must remain present for the full exam duration.

6.2 Leaving early without approval counts as non-participation.

7.Communication & Notifications

7.1 Exam updates will be shared via registered email/SMS.

7.2 Students must regularly check notifications before the exam.

8. Privacy and Data Security

8.1 Personal data collected during the registration process will be used solely for assessment purposes.

8.2 Proctoring recordings will be securely stored and will not be shared with third parties.

9. Penalty for Violations

9.1 Any student found violating the rules will be disqualified immediately and reported to their college authorities.

9.2 Colleges must ensure students understand these rules and take responsibility for violations by their students.

10. Support

10.1 Technical support will be available during the assessment through the provided helpline or email.

10.2 Colleges and students are encouraged to conduct a mock test to familiarize themselves with the process.
